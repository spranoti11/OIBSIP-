K. Sivan

14 April 1957 - Present

Rocketman of India!

Dr. Kailasavadivoo Sivan is an Indian space scientist and the chairperson of the Indian Space Research Organization (ISRO). Earlier he was the director of Vikram Sarabhai Space Center (VSSC) in Kerala state. He worked for many successful missions as a leader, but he came in the spotlight because of the mission Chandryaan 2, which is launched on 24 July 2019 by ISRO. Recently he has been the major part of the successs for the launch of Chandrayan 3 which took place on 23 Aug 2023. Dr.K Sivan was born on 14 April 1957 in Mela Sarakkalvilai, Tamil Nadu, India. He studied in a Tamil medium Government school in Mela Sarakkalvilai Village and later in Vallankumaranvilai, Kanyakumari. Later he did a bachelor’s degree in engineering from Madras Institute of Technology in 1980. Then he did a master’s degree in aerospace engineering from Indian Institute of Science, Bangalore in 1982. Then he did a doctoral degree in aerospace engineering from Indian Institute of Technology, Bombay in 2006.

Awards

  • Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam Award, 2019
  • IEEE Simon Ramo Medal, shared with Byrana N. Suresh, 2020
